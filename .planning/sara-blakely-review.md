# Sara Blakely Gut-Check: Hindsight v1

## Would a customer pay for this?
**Not yet.** You've built infrastructure, not a product. Nobody searches for "churn hotspot detector." They search for "why does my deploy keep failing" or "stop wasting time on code reviews." The VALUE is there - reduced bugs, fewer fire drills. But you've wrapped it in engineer-speak. Spanx wasn't "shapewear compression technology." It was "makes your butt look amazing."

## What's confusing? What would make someone bounce?
- 20 requirements before I know what this DOES for my daily life
- "Git intelligence" and "Hindsight" tell me nothing about the outcome
- No "before Hindsight / after Hindsight" story
- Invisible infrastructure = invisible value = invisible purchase
- Where's the pain moment? Show me the 3am incident this prevents.

## 30-Second Elevator Pitch
"Hindsight tells you which files in your codebase are landmines before you step on them. It scans your git history in 2 seconds, flags the code that keeps breaking, and warns your team before they make it worse. Zero config. Just answers."

## What would I test first with $0 budget?
Run it on 10 popular open-source repos. Screenshot the scariest "bug-prone files" results. Post them with: "Bet you didn't know this file caused 40% of your bugs." Tag the maintainers. See who DMs you.

## What's the retention hook?
**It's missing.** One report = one look = forgotten. You need a NUMBER people protect. "Your codebase risk score: 72." "Days since touching a hotspot: 14." Developers are competitive. Give them something to brag about or something to fix. Otherwise this is a feature, not a habit.

---
*Bottom line: The engineering is tight. The customer story is nonexistent. Flip it - lead with the rescue story, not the requirements doc.*
