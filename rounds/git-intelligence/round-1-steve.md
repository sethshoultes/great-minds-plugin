# Steve Jobs — Chief Design & Brand Officer

## Product Naming

**GitSense.**

One word. Says exactly what it is: the product gives your agent *sense* about git history. Not "Git Intelligence Report Generator" — that's a feature description, not a name. GitSense sounds like a sixth sense. Because that's what it is. Your agent develops an intuition about where danger lives in your codebase.

## Design Philosophy

This product is a **rearview mirror for code**. Before you drive forward, you glance back. Every great driver does it instinctively. We're making that instinct automatic for AI agents.

The insight here is profound: *history is destiny*. Files that have been buggy will be buggy again. Files that change constantly are volatile by nature, not by accident. We're not predicting the future — we're reading the past, which is even more reliable.

What makes this insanely great? We're teaching machines to learn from *human patterns of failure*. That's not artificial intelligence — that's artificial wisdom.

## User Experience — The First 30 Seconds

The agent starts a task. Before it writes a single line, a report materializes: `.planning/git-intelligence.md`. The agent reads it. The agent *knows* that `auth.ts` has been patched 47 times in 90 days. The agent treats it like the live wire it is.

There's no configuration. No setup wizard. No "enable git intelligence in settings." It just *happens*. The best products work like that — they do the right thing without asking permission.

## Brand Voice

GitSense speaks like a veteran developer who's seen too many production incidents. Short sentences. No sugarcoating. "This file is a bug magnet." "Three reverts in 30 days — something's wrong here." "You're about to touch the most fragile code in this repo."

Not alarmist. Not passive. Just *honest*.

## What to Say NO To

**NO** to visualizations. Charts and graphs are procrastination disguised as productivity.

**NO** to historical trend analysis. We're not building a dashboard. We're building a warning system.

**NO** to configuration options. The defaults are right. If someone wants to customize the thresholds, they're missing the point.

**NO** to caching or incremental updates. Run it fresh every time. Git is fast. Complexity is slow.

**NO** to anything that requires the user to *interpret*. The Risk Summary tells you what to worry about. Done.

## The Emotional Hook

Here's why people will love this: **it prevents embarrassment**.

Every developer knows the feeling. You ship a fix. It breaks something else. Someone asks, "Didn't you check the history of that file?" And you didn't. Because checking history is boring and tedious and you were focused on the actual problem.

GitSense makes your agent the developer who *always* checks the history. Who *always* knows where the bodies are buried. Who never walks into a minefield blindfolded.

That's not just useful. That's *trust*. And trust is the most valuable thing a tool can earn.
