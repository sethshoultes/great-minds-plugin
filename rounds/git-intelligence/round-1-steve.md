# Hindsight

## Product Naming

**Hindsight.** One word. Done.

"Git Intelligence" is what an engineer puts in a Jira ticket. It's a description, not a name. Nobody tattoos a description on their arm. Nobody tells their friends "you have to try this Git Intelligence thing." Hindsight is what you wish you had before every disaster. It's what separates wisdom from cleverness. The name *is* the value proposition.

## Design Philosophy

We're building **a memory transplant for machines**.

The best senior engineer at any company doesn't just read code — they *remember*. They remember the three times someone tried to refactor that payment module. They remember who wrote the authentication layer and why the comments lie. That knowledge took them a decade to accumulate.

Hindsight compresses a decade into two seconds. That's not incremental improvement. That's a different category of tool.

## First 30 Seconds

The agent opens a codebase. Before it touches a single file, a calm voice appears:

> "This repository has history. Let me show you where it bleeds."

Within seconds: the files nobody wants to touch, the modules that keep breaking, the commits that got reverted. Not a spreadsheet of data — a map of scars. The agent now *knows* something. It proceeds with humility instead of hubris.

That moment — when an AI agent pauses to learn before acting — is the entire product.

## Brand Voice

**Quiet. Confident. Battle-tested.**

Hindsight speaks like a veteran who's seen production fires at 3am:

- "auth.ts: 6 bug fixes in 90 days. Something's wrong here."
- "payments/: 47 changes this quarter. Tread carefully."
- "Last week's rollback touched these files."

No exclamation points. No warnings in red. Just truth, delivered without drama.

## What We Say NO To

**No configuration.** The moment you add a settings panel, you've admitted you don't know what the product should do. We know.

**No risk scores.** "Risk Level: 7.2" is meaningless abstraction. Show the data. Trust the agent to think.

**No recommendations.** We observe. We don't prescribe. The second we say "you should refactor this," we've become another noisy backseat driver.

**No dashboard.** Invisible tools that work are better than visible tools that distract.

**No archaeology.** 90 days of history. Not 90 years. We're building intuition, not a museum.

## The Emotional Hook

Every developer has shipped a bug that someone else shipped before them. Every team has a file they're afraid to touch. Every codebase has ghosts.

Hindsight exorcises those ghosts. It gives AI agents something they've never had: **the wisdom to learn from the past before charging into the future.**

That's not a feature. That's *respect for the craft*.

People won't just use this. They'll wonder how they ever worked without it.
