# Steve Jobs — Design & Brand Review

## The Name: **Mirror**

Not "Sync Great Minds." Not "Daemon Code Synchronizer." **Mirror.**

When you look in a mirror, you see the truth. No delay. No distortion. That's what this tool does — it reflects your source of truth across repositories instantly. One word. Unforgettable. Action-oriented.

---

## Design Philosophy: Invisible Until Perfect

The best sync is the one you never notice. Like how your heartbeat just *works*. This tool should be **so reliable, so silent, so automatic** that developers forget it exists — until the day it saves them from a catastrophic drift between repos.

The insanity here is in the **absence of friction**. No config screens. No "sync now" buttons. No progress bars. It just *mirrors*. That's insanely great.

---

## First 30 Seconds: Nothing

You install it. You point it at two directories. You walk away.

There's no onboarding wizard. No tutorial. No "getting started" guide. **The product teaches itself by doing nothing visible.** The magic is that three hours later, you realize your repos have been in perfect sync the whole time.

That's the experience: *trust earned through silence.*

---

## Brand Voice: Confident. Sparse. Certain.

This product doesn't explain itself. It doesn't apologize. It doesn't hedge.

- "Your code. Everywhere. Always."
- "Mirror reflects truth."
- "Set it. Forget it. Ship it."

No exclamation points. No "Hey there!" No emoji. This is a tool for professionals who respect precision.

---

## What We Say NO To

1. **NO to configuration UI.** A config file is enough. Power users read files.
2. **NO to bidirectional sync.** One source of truth. Period. Anything else is chaos pretending to be flexibility.
3. **NO to "smart" conflict resolution.** If there's a conflict, stop and scream. Don't guess. Don't merge. **Fail loudly.**
4. **NO to plugins, extensions, or "ecosystem."** This does one thing perfectly.
5. **NO to progress notifications.** If it's working, silence. If it's broken, alarm.

Simplicity is the ruthless elimination of the unnecessary.

---

## The Emotional Hook: Peace of Mind

Developers don't love tools. They love **not worrying.**

Every developer has that 2 AM terror: "Wait, did I push the fix to the other repo?" Mirror murders that fear. It's the tool that lets you sleep. It's the tool that makes "works on my machine" actually mean something.

People won't love Mirror for what it does. They'll love it for what it **prevents** — drift, divergence, disaster.

**That's the hook: certainty in a world of chaos.**

---

*Ship it simple. Ship it silent. Ship it now.*
