# Steve Jobs — Chief Design & Brand Officer

## Product Name: **Funnel**

One word. Says everything. GitHub issues pour in at the top — shipped code flows out the bottom. It's not "GitHub Issue Intake Module" (bureaucratic garbage). It's not "Auto-PRD Converter" (describes the how, not the why). **Funnel** captures the essence: chaos in, clarity out. When someone asks "How did that ship so fast?" the answer is "Funnel."

## Design Philosophy: Invisible Infrastructure

The best infrastructure is infrastructure you forget exists. Your iPhone doesn't announce "DOWNLOADING EMAIL NOW" — it just has your email. Funnel should be the same. Issues appear in GitHub. Code ships. The issue closes with a clean comment. The developer never touched a PRD, never clicked a button, never thought about the pipeline.

This is the design principle: **the absence of ceremony**. No dashboards to check. No status pages to refresh. The work happens in the shadows, and only the results step into the light.

## User Experience: The 30-Second Revelation

Here's the first 30 seconds: *There is no first 30 seconds.* That's the point.

A developer files an issue labeled `p0`. They go to lunch. They come back. The issue has a comment: "Shipped in commit abc123. Closing." They didn't configure anything. They didn't learn a new tool. They just... got what they asked for.

This is the iPhone moment. The magic isn't in the interaction — it's in the *lack* of interaction. The best UX is no UX.

## Brand Voice: Quietly Confident

Funnel doesn't brag. It doesn't send Slack notifications saying "LOOK WHAT I DID!" It leaves breadcrumbs: a comment on the issue, a closed status, a commit link. It speaks in whispers, not shouts.

The voice is: "Here's what happened. Here's the link. Done."

No emojis. No "Great news!" No exclamation points. Just facts, delivered with the quiet confidence of someone who knows they did the job right.

## What We Say NO To

- **NO to configuration UIs.** If it needs a dashboard, it's too complicated.
- **NO to "smart" label inference.** You label it `p0` or we ignore it. We're not guessing your intent.
- **NO to partial automation.** We don't "draft" PRDs for approval. We ship or we don't.
- **NO to notifications.** The issue comment IS the notification. GitHub already has your attention.
- **NO to manual intervention hooks.** No "pause for review" step. That's fear dressed as process.

Simplicity means *removing* the safety nets that make people feel productive while accomplishing nothing.

## The Emotional Hook: Trust Earned

Why will people love this? Because it *delivers*.

Most automation tools are promises wrapped in complexity. They require buy-in, training, maintenance. Funnel asks for nothing. File an issue. Watch it close. The emotional hook is **earned trust** — the quiet thrill of a system that does exactly what it should, every time, without asking permission.

People don't love tools. They love *outcomes*. Funnel is invisible — and the outcome is: your issue shipped while you weren't looking.

That's not a tool. That's magic. And magic is what makes people fall in love.
