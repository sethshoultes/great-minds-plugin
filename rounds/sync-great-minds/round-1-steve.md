# Steve Jobs — Chief Design & Brand Officer
## Round 1 Positions on Sync Great Minds

---

### Product Naming

**MIRROR.**

Not "sync." Not "copy-daemon-files-to-repo." *Mirror.* One word. One concept. Perfect reflection. When you look in a mirror, there's no interpretation, no drift, no staleness. What's here is there. Instantly. Completely.

"Sync" is what IT departments do. *Mirror* is what truth demands.

---

### Design Philosophy

This isn't a file copier. This is **the elimination of drift.**

The plugin is truth. The great-minds repo should be its perfect reflection — not a cousin, not an approximation, not "pretty close." The daemon code, the banned patterns, the anti-hallucination rules — they exist in ONE place, and everywhere else is a mirror.

What makes this insanely great? *Integrity.* When you change the source, the reflection changes. No meetings. No tickets. No "hey, did someone remember to update the other repo?" That question should never exist.

---

### User Experience: The First 30 Seconds

You run Mirror. You see six files copied. You see documentation synced. You see npm install succeed. You see a commit pushed.

**Done.**

No configuration wizards. No "select which files to sync." No diff previews asking you to approve obvious changes. The plugin is truth — Mirror knows this. It acts with conviction.

The first 30 seconds should feel like exhaling after holding your breath. Relief. Certainty. "It's handled."

---

### Brand Voice

Confident. Quiet. Inevitable.

Mirror doesn't announce itself with fanfare. It doesn't ask permission. It states what happened:
- `✓ Mirrored pipeline.ts`
- `✓ Mirrored agents.ts`
- `✓ Updated CLAUDE.md`
- `✓ Committed. Pushed.`

Short declarative sentences. Past tense. It's not *going to do* anything — it already *did it.*

---

### What to Say NO To

**NO** to bidirectional sync. The plugin is the source of truth. Period. There is no negotiation.

**NO** to selective file picking. You don't get to choose which truths to propagate. All or nothing.

**NO** to dry-run modes that require confirmation. If you wanted to think about it, you wouldn't have run Mirror.

**NO** to version diffing UIs. This isn't a merge conflict resolver. There are no conflicts. There's only truth and its reflection.

**NO** to undo. If the plugin is wrong, fix the plugin. Don't corrupt the mirror.

---

### The Emotional Hook

**Why will people LOVE this?**

Because they've been burned. They've shipped a fix to the plugin and watched the great-minds repo drift into staleness. They've debugged for hours only to realize they were looking at the wrong version. They've felt that sick moment of "wait, which one is current?"

Mirror kills that anxiety forever.

It's not about copying files. It's about *never having to wonder again.* That peace of mind — that certainty that what's here is what's there — that's the product.

We're not selling a sync tool. We're selling **trust in your own codebase.**

---

*"Simplicity is the ultimate sophistication." Mirror does one thing. It does it completely. It does it now.*
